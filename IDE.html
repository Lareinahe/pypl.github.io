FAQ

What is the PYIDE index ?

The PYIDE PopularitY of IDE Index is created by analyzing how often IDEs are searched on Google : the more an IDE is searched, the more popular the language is assumed to be. The raw data comes from Google Trends.

If you believe in collective wisdom, the PYIDE Popularity of IDE index can help you decide which IDE to use for your software development project.

My favorite IDE is not in the index ! Why ?

Some IDE are not listed because their name has other meanings, or because there is not enough data for them on Google Trends.

How do you compute the share of web searches ?

We export the data from Google Trends in CSV format, [s] and analyze it with pyDatalog. We first calculate the interest of each IDE relative to Visual Studio every month. Normalizing the total to 100% yields the share of interest in each IDE, i.e. their popularity, which we smooth over 6 months. Linear regression over the last 12 months gives the yearly trends.
